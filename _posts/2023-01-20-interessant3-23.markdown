[The history of the exocentric compound noun](https://substack.com/redirect/a3f344d8-924c-4ac6-95c7-f3ec03c27654?r=b5lkt) : although English usually combines verbs and nouns in as $NOUN-$VERBER (eg “firefighter”, “giftgiver”), some lower-class people between 1550 and 1700 experimented with an alternative form, $VERB\_$NOUN. Their dialect survives in a few words most relevant to seedy 17th century life, like “pickpocket”, “turncoat”, “cutthroat”, and “sellsword.
