1. Considered one of the top 10 Global Health Threats by the WHO, Dengue fever is a disease endemic to over 100 countries, and spreading. Globally reported cases increased from 500,000/year in 2000 to 5 million in in 2019, with modelling estimating the real figure to be a staggering 390 million infections a year, and about 36,000 deaths. Worryingly, there was no Dengue-specific treatment, until very recently. Japanese pharma company Takeda has developed a Dengue vaccine, which in studies has prevented 84% of hospitalizations and 61% of symptomatic Dengue illness overall, with no identified important safety risks.
