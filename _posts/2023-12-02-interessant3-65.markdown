1.  **Remembering Charlie Munger: The Wisdom of 'Inversion'.** The recent passing of Charlie Munger, the legendary investor and partner of Warren Buffett, marks the end of an era in the world of finance. Munger was renowned for his sharp wit and profound insights, notably his advocacy of the mental model of 'inversion'. This approach, where one considers the opposite of what they want to avoid, helped shape much of his investment strategy. His legacy continues to inspire and guide investors around the world. 2.  **Revisiting the 20th Century: Inequality Remains Constant?** A groundbreaking study in a top peer-reviewed journal challenges the widely-accepted views of economist Thomas Piketty on 20th-century inequality. Contrary to Piketty's findings, this new research suggests that US real income has increased for all groups and inequality levels remained consistent throughout the century. This revelation could reshape our understanding of economic history and prompt a reevaluation of current economic policies.
