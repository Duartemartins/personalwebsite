###  **The Endlessly Mysterious Eel**

For centuries, the life cycle of eels baffled scientists - Aristotle thought they emerged from mud, and Sigmund Freud once dissected them in search of sex organs. The truth? All European and American freshwater eels are born in the Sargasso Sea, an area of the Atlantic Ocean off the Caribbean and North American coasts first discovered by Portuguese sailors, before migrating thousands of miles to rivers and lakes, where they live for decades before returning to spawn and die.

- Eels start life in the Sargasso Sea, drifting towards Europe and North America as transparent larvae.
- They spend up to 85 years in freshwater before transforming for their final trip back.

###  **Theanine: A Brain Booster, or Just Hype?**

Theanine, an amino acid found in tea, is widely believed to promote calm, focus, and reduced anxiety. But does it actually work? put it to the test in a 16-month placebo-controlled self-experiment - and the results were surprising.

- The science is weak - reviews from major health authorities, including the European Food Safety Authority, found no strong evidence that theanine improves mood or cognition.
- Placebo effect in action? Despite meticulous tracking, the biohacker found that stress levels dropped just as much whether he took theanine or a vitamin D placebo.
- Expectation vs. reality - he was confident he could tell the difference, but in nearly 100 blind trials, he guessed wrong just as often as chance.
- A challenge to believers - many swear by theanine, but if it truly works, it should hold up under blind testing - and this experiment suggests it might not.
- The takeaway: Theanine might feel effective, but this test raises serious doubts. If you‚Äôre convinced it helps, the challenge is simple: try a blind test yourself.
